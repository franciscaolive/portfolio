Glitch Box

Glitch Box is an interactive installation, inspired by the Nintendo game, Super Mario, that allows for the spectator to interact with a 3D asset, a surprise box. Every time the spectator touches this box, it plays a sound and disappears, appearing elsewhere, ready to repeat that event. The box can randomly appear in any part of the screen. and the spectator has to move around the entire room to be able to successfuly play.

My colleague Alice Logvina and I developed this project together under the guidance of Professor Tiago Dionísio. We chose to use a Kinect in combination with Max and Processing, and created a patch that communicates via OSC. In Max, we were able to associate spheres with the hands, which allowed us to detect movements and generate real-time interactions. We integrated the 3D patch with the full scene, including elements like the castle, clouds, and the 'mystery box', which became the centerpiece of the interactive experience. We worked on the collision between objects and the hands, creating distinct sound responses for each one, and adjusted the movement axes for greater realism. To improve the scene's lighting, we explored Max's documentation and added light parameters that made the environment brighter and more immersive. The clouds were animated to bring more dynamism to the installation, and to complete the experience, we decided to include the Super Mario music, which plays on a loop throughout the interaction.
